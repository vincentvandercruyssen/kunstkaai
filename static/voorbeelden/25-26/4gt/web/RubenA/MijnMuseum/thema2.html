Films and Series

Films

Waves is a drama about a family dealing with pressure, mistakes, and healing. It follows a teenage boy named Tyler, who feels intense pressure from his strict father to succeed in wrestling and life. As his stress builds—along with relationship problems and a serious injury—he makes a series of bad decisions that lead to a tragic turning point.After this, the film shifts focus to his younger sister, Emily. While the family struggles with grief and guilt, Emily tries to move forward, find love, and bring some sense of peace back into their lives.

The Hunger Games is a story set in a dystopian future where the country of Panem forces children from its districts to compete in a deadly televised event called the Hunger Games. The film follows Katniss Everdeen, a teenage girl who volunteers to take her sister’s place in the Games. Along with another tribute from her district, Peeta Mellark, she is sent to fight against other teenagers in an arena where only one person can survive. While in the Games, Katniss uses her survival skills, intelligence, and courage to stay alive. At the same time, she begins to challenge the cruel system controlled by the Capitol, led by President Snow.

Series

Snowfall is a gripping crime drama that explores the early days of the crack cocaine epidemic in Los Angeles during the 1980s. Created by John Singleton along with Eric Amadio and Dave Andron, the series blends fictional storytelling with real historical context to show how the drug trade reshaped communities and fueled systemic issues in the United States.The story follows Franklin Saint, a young and ambitious man who becomes deeply involved in the rising drug business, alongside a diverse cast of characters whose lives intersect in unexpected ways. These include a CIA operative entangled in covert operations, a Mexican wrestler turned enforcer.

Breaking Bad is a critically acclaimed crime drama that tells the story of Walter White, a high school chemistry teacher turned methamphetamine manufacturer. Created by Vince Gilligan, the series is widely regarded as one of the greatest TV shows of all time for its intense storytelling, complex characters, and moral exploration. The show begins with Walter White being diagnosed with terminal lung cancer, which propels him to use his chemistry skills to produce high-quality meth in order to secure his family's financial future. As Walter partners with former student Jesse Pinkman, their operation grows, but so do the consequences of their choices.
